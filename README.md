# Maven Telecom Customer Churn Analysis

## Project Overview
End-to-end business intelligence project analysing customer churn for Maven communication, a fictional California-based telecom operator. Built to identify high-risk customer segments, quantify revenue impact, and deliver actionable retention recommendations to business stakeholders.

## Business Problem
Maven Communication lost 1,869 customers in Q2 2022 - a 26.54% churn rate costing the business $3.68M in revenue. With no structured view of who is leaving, why they are leaving, or what it costs, the Head of Customer Retention commissioned this analysis to identify root causes and prioritise retention spend.

## Dataset
- **Source:** Maven Analytics Data Playground - Telecom Customer Churn
- **Size:** 7,043 customers · Q2 2022 · California
- **Raw file:** single flat table, `telecom_customer_churn.csv`
- **Link:** https://mavenanalytics.io/data-playground

## Data Model
The raw dataset arrives as one flat table. It was split into 5 normalised tables to demonstrate SQL JOIN skills and mirror real-world database design - 4 connect back via `customer_id`, 1 via `zip_code`:
- **customer** - demographics, tenure, referrals, offer *(customer_id)*
- **payment** - charges, revenue, contract, billing method *(customer_id)*
- **services** - all service subscriptions (phone, internet, streaming, security) *(customer_id)*
- **customer_churn** - churn status, category, reason *(customer_id)*
- **geolocation** - city, zip code, coordinates, population *(zip_code)*

## Data Quality Notes
- 120 records (1.7%) originally had a negative `monthly_charge` value (billing credits/system errors). These were cleaned to 0 using Power Query before the data was loaded into BigQuery
- NULL values in `churn_reason` and `churn_category` are intentional - only applicable to churned customers
- Senior citizen defined as Age >= 65 (WHO global standard)
- **High Value Customer definition:** monthly charge above the customer-base average AND tenure above the customer-base average. This average is calculated across the whole customer base, including churned customers, so it's a reasonable but not the only valid threshold - using an active-customers-only baseline instead shifts the high value count by roughly 14%

## Key Findings

1. **Maven communication lost 26.54% of customers in Q2 2022** - 1,869 customers, tied to $3.68M in churned revenue (17.24% of total Q2 revenue). This is historical revenue generated by customers who later left, not a forward-looking loss projection

2. **Churn splits into what Maven can't control and what it can.** Competitors caused $1.69M in churned revenue (45% of total) - driven mainly by "better devices" (313 customers) and "better offers" (311 customers), money that's genuinely hard to win back on price alone. But $1.2M came from problems Maven created itself: poor support staff attitude ($0.58M) and product dissatisfaction ($0.62M). That's the money worth fixing first, since it doesn't require beating a competitor's price - just fixing what's broken internally

3. **Month-to-Month customers churn at 45.84% vs 2.55% for Two-Year contracts** - 88.55% of all churned customers had no long-term commitment to Maven

4. **Customers in their first 12 months churn at 47.44%** - churn drops consistently with each year of tenure, falling to 11.93% after 3+ years

5. **Senior + Single + No Dependents churn at 48.92%** - highest of any demographic combination vs Married + Dependents at just 3.88%. Customers with people depending on them - a spouse, children - are far more likely to stay than someone with no one relying on their service staying active

6. **Online Security and Tech Support reduce churn by nearly 3x** - subscribed customers churn at ~15% vs ~41% for non-subscribers (27-point gap). Streaming services show only a 3-4 point gap - no meaningful loyalty value

7. **Churn is geographically concentrated** - San Diego (185 churned, 49 high value) and Los Angeles (78 churned, 16 high value) alone account for a disproportionate share of losses, followed by Escondido (16 churned, 5 high value), making city-level targeting viable for retention campaigns

8. **Fiber Optic customers drive two-thirds of all churn** - 1,236 of the 1,869 total churned customers (66.14%) were on Fiber Optic internet, far outweighing their 43% share of the customer base. Fiber Optic customers also churn at more than double the rate of other internet types (40.72% vs 18-26%), confirming this isn't just a base-size effect. This points to either a service quality gap on the Fiber Optic network or a competitor targeting pattern

## Business Recommendations

1. **Marketing Department - Contract Conversion Campaign**
   Target Month-to-Month customers with incentivised annual plan offers. Converting even 20% to One-Year contracts could reduce churn rate from 45.84% to closer to 10.71%, directly protecting $1.69M at competitive risk.

2. **Customer Service Department - Support Quality Programme**
   "Attitude of support person" caused 220 churns and $404K in churned revenue - the #3 churn reason and highest internally controllable one. A support staff retraining programme targeting phone and online support quality could recover this segment within one quarter.

3. **Product & Marketing Department - Service Bundling Strategy**
   Bundle Online Security and Tech Support into every base plan - stop selling them as premium add-ons. These two services cut churn by nearly 3x. Offer them proactively to every month-to-month, inactive, or single-product customer to bring down early-tenure churn.

4. **Retention Team - City-Targeted Campaigns**
   Focus retention outreach on the top churn cities (San Diego, Los Angeles, Escondido) where both overall and high-value churn are concentrated, rather than spreading budget evenly across all locations.

5. **Network & Product Team - Investigate Fiber Optic Churn**
   With 66.14% of all churn coming from Fiber Optic customers - who also churn at more than double the rate of other internet types (40.72% vs 18-26%) - investigate whether this comes from network reliability issues or targeted competitor pricing at higher-bill Fiber customers, then act on the confirmed root cause.

## Retention ROI (Power BI - Retention Target Page)
At default settings ($150 offer per customer, 40% retention success rate):
- **Target segment:** 351 high value churned customers
- **Estimated annual revenue at risk:** $403.20K (12-month forward projection based on monthly billing, not historical lifetime revenue)
- **Campaign cost:** $52K
- **Revenue recovered:** ~$161.28K
- **Net ROI:** $108.93K (~3x return)

Break-even retention success rate: ~12.9%
